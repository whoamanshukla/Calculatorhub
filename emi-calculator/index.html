How EMI Is Calculated

A simple explanation of the EMI formula used by the calculator.

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay your loan over the selected tenure. Each EMI usually includes both a portion of the principal amount and the interest amount.

The EMI is calculated using a standard formula that considers the loan amount (principal), the monthly interest rate, and the total number of monthly installments.

Formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1 + R)N] / [(1 + R)N - 1]

Where:

  • P = Loan amount
  • R = Monthly interest rate
  • N = Number of monthly installments

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about this EMI calculator.

What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed monthly amount you pay to repay a loan over a chosen period.

How do I calculate EMI for a loan?

Enter your loan amount, annual interest rate and loan tenure in years. Then click the Calculate EMI button to get the result instantly.

Can I use this EMI calculator for home loans?

Yes. You can use it for home loans, car loans, personal loans and other common loans.

Does a longer loan tenure reduce EMI?

Yes, a longer tenure generally reduces the monthly EMI, but it can increase the total interest paid.
